Wonder Woman (2017)

84%

A confident, emotionally grounded superhero origin story that gave DC its much-needed win.

Genre: Action, Fantasy
Director: Patty Jenkins
Runtime: 2h 21m

Critic Review

Wonder Woman marked a turning point for DC films. Unlike its darker predecessors, the film embraces hope, sincerity, and emotional storytelling. Gal Gadot brings both strength and compassion to the role, making Diana Prince a genuinely inspiring hero.

Patty Jenkins directs the film with clarity and confidence, balancing large-scale action with quieter, character-driven moments. The World War I setting adds thematic weight, helping the film stand apart from typical superhero fare.

What Works

  • Strong lead performance by Gal Gadot
  • Clear emotional arc and character growth
  • Effective blend of action and drama
  • Memorable musical score

What Doesn’t

  • Predictable third-act villain
  • Some uneven CGI during the climax

Final Verdict

Wonder Woman succeeds by focusing on character over spectacle. It remains one of the most satisfying DC films and a benchmark for female-led superhero movies.
